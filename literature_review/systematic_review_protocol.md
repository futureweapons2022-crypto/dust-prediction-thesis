# Systematic Literature Review Protocol — DRAFT (Google Scholar)

## Research Question
"Has anyone used machine learning to predict when dust forecasting models will fail, and has anyone integrated anthropogenic land-use signals into dust forecast error prediction?"

## Sub-questions
1. Has ML been used to predict dust forecast MODEL ERROR (not dust itself)?
2. Have anthropogenic land-use changes been linked to dust forecast performance?
3. Have LLMs/NLP been used for environmental event extraction and integration into atmospheric models?

## Search Strategy

### Database
- Google Scholar (draft — to be supplemented with Scopus + Web of Science for final thesis)

### Search Queries
| ID | Query String | Target |
|----|-------------|--------|
| S1 | "dust forecast" AND ("model error" OR "forecast error" OR "forecast failure" OR "bias correction") AND "machine learning" | ML for dust forecast error |
| S2 | "meta-model" AND "dust" AND ("forecast" OR "prediction") | Meta-modeling for dust |
| S3 | "anthropogenic" AND "dust" AND ("land use" OR "land degradation") AND ("forecast" OR "model") | Anthropogenic signals in dust models |
| S4 | "large language model" AND ("atmospheric" OR "dust" OR "aerosol" OR "environmental") AND ("extraction" OR "mining") | LLMs in atmospheric science |
| S5 | "CAMS" AND "dust" AND ("error" OR "bias" OR "evaluation") AND "AERONET" | CAMS dust evaluation specifically |
| S6 | "predict" AND "when" AND "model" AND "fail" AND ("dust" OR "aerosol" OR "weather") | Forecast failure prediction general |

### Inclusion Criteria
- Published 2015–2026
- Peer-reviewed or preprint (arXiv)
- English language
- Addresses dust/aerosol forecasting, model evaluation, or ML for atmospheric prediction
- Relevant to at least one sub-question

### Exclusion Criteria
- Pure dust detection/classification from satellite imagery (not forecast error)
- Indoor air quality / occupational dust
- Pure climate projection without forecast evaluation
- Reviews/meta-analyses (noted separately)
- Not accessible (title/abstract only, no information extractable)
